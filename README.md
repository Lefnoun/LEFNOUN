## PDIF Engineering Architecture

PDIF is being developed around a structured engineering intelligence architecture.

### Engineering Knowledge and Data

- **EDB — Engineering Database**  
  Preserves engineering experience, historical problems, design observations, and previously validated engineering solutions.

- **DDB — Diagnosis Database**  
  Captures diagnosed implementation issues, root causes, symptoms, and engineering relationships.

- **TDB — Trial Database**  
  Records engineering trials, implementation changes, experimental iterations, and their measured impact on the design.

- **RDB — Rule Database**  
  Maintains engineering rules, diagnosis logic, design constraints, and validated decision knowledge.

### Intelligence Systems

- **Engineering Knowledge Graph**  
  Models relationships between design stages, physical structures, implementation symptoms, engineering actions, and design outcomes.

- **Diagnosis Engine**  
  Performs systematic root-cause analysis of physical design implementation issues.

- **Decision Engine**  
  Evaluates engineering evidence and recommends corrective actions across the physical design flow.

- **Digital Twin**  
  Maintains a contextual representation of the evolving physical design state and its cross-stage dependencies.

## Engineering Philosophy

A timing violation may not originate from timing optimization.

Its true source may exist in:

- Synthesis
- Constraints
- Floorplanning
- Pad Placement
- Macro Placement
- Power Planning
- Congestion
- Clock Architecture
- Routing
- Physical Design Methodology

PDIF is designed to investigate the engineering source of a problem before recommending corrective action.

> **Understand the source. Diagnose the problem. Make the engineering decision.**
